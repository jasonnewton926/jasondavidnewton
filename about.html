About Jason David Newton

Moses Lake, Washington

I’m a mechanical and operations professional based in Moses Lake, Washington with more than a decade of experience working in manufacturing, industrial maintenance, and maritime engineering environments.

Professional Background

I graduated from Washington State University in 2016 with a Bachelor of Arts in Business Management & Operations. My education provided the foundation for understanding business systems, operations strategy, and leadership.

Over the course of my career I’ve worked in demanding industrial environments where reliability, safety, and teamwork are essential. My experience includes maintenance operations, mechanical systems, fabrication work, and leading teams responsible for maintaining critical production equipment.

I currently work as a Mechanical Technician at Group14 Technologies in Moses Lake where I focus on maintenance, repairs, and fabrication while also supporting planning and scheduling efforts within the maintenance organization.

Maritime Engineering Experience

In addition to my industrial career, I earned a U.S. Coast Guard Merchant Mariner Credential as a Qualified Member of the Engineering Department (QMED) through Mid-Atlantic Maritime Academy in Norfolk, Virginia.

During my time working aboard commercial fishing vessels I was responsible for maintaining and operating complex mechanical systems including large-scale refrigeration plants, boilers, diesel-electric power systems, and onboard fish processing equipment.

This experience strengthened my technical abilities and reinforced the importance of teamwork, discipline, and reliability when operating mission-critical equipment in challenging environments.

Leadership & Community

Throughout my career I have had the opportunity to lead teams in both operational and maintenance environments. While working at Simplot FG in Moses Lake I supervised teams of 30–50 personnel responsible for production, safety, maintenance coordination, and operational efficiency.

Outside of work I believe strongly in investing in community and helping others grow. I previously volunteered as a middle school football coach in Moses Lake and continue to stay involved in my local church community.

I am a member of Grace Harvest Church where I attend regularly and continue to grow in my faith and service to others.

Faith, Family, and Personal Life

The most important role in my life is being a father. Raising my son and setting a strong example for him is something that motivates me every day.

My faith in Jesus Christ shapes how I approach life, work, and relationships. I believe in striving to grow every day as a man, a father, and a member of my community.

Outside of work I enjoy lifting weights, golf, snowboarding, watching great movies, listening to music, reading, and studying the Bible.
