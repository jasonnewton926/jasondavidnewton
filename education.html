My Educational Journey in Moses Lake, Washington

As a Mechanical Technician at Group 14 Technologies in Moses Lake, Washington, my education has been foundational to my career in operations, maintenance, and leadership. From business principles to hands-on maritime training, these experiences inform my daily work while aligning with my faith at Grace Harvest Church and commitment to family. See how this ties into my community involvement.

Educational Milestones

Year Credential/Degree Institution Key Skills & Insights
2010-2013 Associate of Arts and Sciences Big Bend Community College, Moses Lake, WA Big Bend's AAS is basically a transfer degree that lets you knock out all the gen ed stuff – math, science, writing – so you can move on to a four-year school without missing a beat. It's practical for locals like me, and it built that broad base I needed for business classes later on. Helped me transition smoothly while staying close to home.
2013-2016 Bachelor of Arts in Business Management & Operations Washington State University, Pullman, WA WSU's Carson College program focuses on things like supply chain, operations strategy, and leading teams – it's all about making businesses run better. The coursework was hands-on, with projects that mirrored real-world challenges, which has been huge for my roles in manufacturing and tech. Tied in nicely with my values from Grace Harvest Church, emphasizing ethical leadership.
2020-2021 U.S. Coast Guard Merchant Mariner Credential (Qualified Member of the Engine Department - QMED) with Electrician and Refrigerating Engineer endorsements Mid-Atlantic Maritime Academy, Virginia Beach, VA MAMA offers USCG-approved training that's intense but effective – covering marine engineering basics, electrical systems, refrigeration, and safety protocols. The QMED credential, with those endorsements, qualified me for engine room duties on vessels, and it's all practical, with simulations and real equipment. It's shaped how I handle high-stakes maintenance, bringing that discipline back to my work in Moses Lake. Photos from that time in my gallery.

How Education Shapes My Life

Education's more than just the degrees – it's the lessons that stick with you over time. My AAS from Big Bend gave me that solid start, WSU built on it with business smarts for leading teams, and the maritime credential from MAMA added the technical grit for fixing things under pressure. All of it blends with my faith and family life here in Moses Lake, helping me mentor others and stay grounded.
